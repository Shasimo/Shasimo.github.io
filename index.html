Introduction

What are Portalgons? First let us define some basic notions of Portalgons. Portalgons are essentially a collection of simple polygons or any shapes having an intrinsic polyhedral metric and are represented as flattened on the 2D plane by a series of their constituting fragments where any edge of such decomposition can be part of a so called portal. A portal is a couple of oriented edges that define a communicating pair of fragments. A fragment is a polygon constituting your portalgon in which any cut once subtracted is a new fragment, creates portals along the edges your cut created, where any portal shares one side on the freshly cut piece, and your cut base polygon. meaning you can seemlessly walk through portal edges to end up in another fragment of your portalgon. We here limit ourselves to 1-happy portalgons whose fragments are simple polygons. The happiness describes the number of times we allow ourselves to cross a portal. We describe later on why such limitation is necessary.

\[ n! \sim \sqrt{2 \pi n} \left( \frac{n}{e} \right)^n \quad \text{as} \quad n \to \infty \]
